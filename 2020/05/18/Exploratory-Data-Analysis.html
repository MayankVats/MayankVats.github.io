Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

18 May 2020 - Mayank Vats

The first and important step after all the relevant data has been collected, stored and loaded is to analyse and explore the dataset.

But “Why” do we need to explore the data? It is like asking why do we pick up the best and fresh apples for making apple juice if bad ones does the same work.

Exploratory Data Analysis or EDA helps us identify what apples to pick and which ones to clean, in data terminology, EDA helps in identifying the bad columns, (with missing value, noise, typing errors or any inconsistencies), that may have occured while collecting data.

How does EDA work, What are the steps?

  • Step 1: Know the shape of the datasets(number of columns and rows)
  • Step 2: Peek some of the values of the dataset
  • Step 3: Get the summary of the numerical columns.
  • Step 4: Visualize and compare the number of missing values of different columns.
